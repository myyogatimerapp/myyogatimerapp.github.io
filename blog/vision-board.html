Vision Board

A powerful tool to keep you on track.

Posted by MyYogaTimer App on September 9, 2020

Have you ever heard the phrase 'Keep you eye on the prize'?

Why is it we have to keep our eye on it? If we want it, surely we don't need reminding of it? The fact is, our mind isn't wired for long term thinking. It likes short term gains like comfort & societal acceptance. If we're not contantly reminding ourselves of a bigger long term vision for ourselves, our mind will default to the easier, well-worn path. If we haven't been using our body in recent time or have accumulated habits stopping us, we are going to have a hard time breaking this if an alternative isn't provided.

We have to actively contribute an alternative story to follow if we want a say in our future. One powerful tool to do this is a Vision Board. This is a collage of people, events or things to move toward. By constantly revisiting our vision board, we can help remind our mind of the future we want to create. In this post I'll cover five tips to get the most out of your vision board.

Stick to People not events or things

A vision board works by reminding us of where we want to go. This creates a drive within us to do better & expect more from ourselves in order to reach this state. If this drive comes from intrinsic motivation it's going to last longer & work at a deeper level than extrinsic motivation - motivation based on external rewards. If we chose people as our future vision, we're playing the infinte game. There is no end win condition. It's striving for the 'state of being' these people embody. Not achieving the things they have achieved, but being someone who is capable of achieving these things. Notice the difference.

Envision being the people you see

See yourself as the people you admire, doing the things they're doing. You are capable of doing these things. It's about reminding your brain this is who you are and where you want to go. Let your brain know what it is you want so it can rise up to these expectations & support you. See yourself literally in their shoes.

Visit your board when you wake up

We are most primed for influence when we wake up in the morning. It's like loading in the program the rest of your day is going to run on. Use this to your advantage & prime your mind with the future you see. If you haven't provided an alternative, our brains default to short term desires and habits. By priming our mind with a longer term vision as soon as we wake up, we can guide the day towards the future we want.

Visit your board whenever you lose vision throughout the day

Notice when you lose vision throughout the day. Maybe you lack energy for no particular reason, you're inundated with heavy emotional thoughts or you feel a lack of purpose. This is evidence you've lost sight of your long term vision. Revisit your board to gain clarity. This is important as a lack of vision & clarity can prevent the energy we need to do yoga & movement from being accessed.

Visit your board before bed

Prime your mind to align with our vision before you go to bed. What we process during sleep can be influenced by what we process leading up to sleep. By providing a framework (our vision) in which the day's events can be process through, the more we will lean towards our future vision.

Conclusion

What's this go to do with doing yoga? Ultimately, things like yoga, exercise & movement need some longer term vision behind them to make them appealing. If there isn't a deeper reason to get up and do yoga over watching tv or staying in bed, we're going to do the latter. We have to be active in creating a longer term vision to strive for. We can't expect it to land on our laps, & we don't want to leave it up to chance. We are able to shape what we want from life.
